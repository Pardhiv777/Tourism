About Darjeeling

Darjeeling, often referred to as the "Queen of the Hills," is a breathtaking hill station in West Bengal, India. Nestled in the Lesser Himalayas at an elevation of 6,700 feet, it is renowned for its panoramic views of the Kanchenjunga mountain range, lush tea gardens, and vibrant culture. Darjeeling is a paradise for nature lovers, adventure enthusiasts, and those seeking tranquility.

The town is famous for its Darjeeling Himalayan Railway, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and its world-class tea, often referred to as the "Champagne of Teas." Visitors can explore its colonial architecture, ancient monasteries, and vibrant markets. Whether you're trekking through the Himalayas, sipping tea at a local estate, or riding the iconic Toy Train, Darjeeling offers an unforgettable experience.
